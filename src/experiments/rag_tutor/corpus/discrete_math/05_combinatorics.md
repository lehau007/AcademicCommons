# Generalized Product Rule

The generalized product rule states that if a procedure consists of sequential tasks T1, T2, ..., Tk that can be done in m1, m2, ..., mk ways respectively, then there are m1 * m2 * ... * mk ways to carry out the procedure. This rule is the foundation of most counting arguments and underlies the formulas for permutations and combinations.

# Pigeonhole Principle

The Dirichlet principle, also known as the Pigeonhole principle, is formally stated as follows: if more than n objects are put into n boxes, then at least one box has at least 2 objects. Despite its simplicity, the Pigeonhole principle proves surprisingly strong results across combinatorics, number theory, and computer science.

# Big Oh Notation

The Big Oh (O) notation classifies functions by their asymptotic growth rate. Intuitively, the Big Oh notation O(g(n)) represents the set of all functions whose rate of growth is the same as or lower than that of g(n). Big Oh provides an upper bound; the related notations Omega and Theta provide lower and tight bounds respectively.
